Mining ER Data Project

Analyzing UCI Emergency Department (ED) patient data to find leading causes of ED frequent flyers in order to decrease ED overcrowding.

Learn More

The Problem With ED Frequent Flyers

Frequent flyers are one of the principal causes of overcrowding in US emergency departments (EDs). Frequent flyers are patients who visit the ED for treatment 4+ times a year. It is not clear why frequent flyers visit the ED so often, but it is also highly unlikely that any patient will face a life or death emergency situation 4+ times a year.

Their visits to the ED may be due to both a combination of intentional and unintentional misuse possibly because of a lack of proper insurance or adequate medical knowledge. They may be homeless or chronically ill; they may also be malingering. Regardless of their reasons, frequent flyers consume valuable ED resources. Moreover, their dependency on the ED often prevents them from seeking out other options that could be more tailored and beneficial to their health needs.

Our Solution

Proposed Innovation

Our clients will supply us with a list of unsorted, but structured ED patient data and we will use the data to create clusters of anywhere from 2-5 groups of frequent flyers with similar needs. The following is the process we are taking to approach the problem.

Research

Find and read literature reviews to narrow down popular characteristics found between ED frequent flyers.

Evaluate

Identify existing solutions and analyze gaps for how other EDs addressed frequent flyers.

Data Analysis

Sort through a list of structured UCI ED patient data to find possible correlations between frequent flyers and identify groups with similar needs.

Propose Solutions

Devise targeted solutions for groups of frequent flyers in order to help reduce UCI ED overcrowding.

Clients

Our clients are the UCI Emergency Department, and specifically: Andrew Wong M.D. and Maxwell Jen M.D.

Our Project

For our project, we identified and categorized UCI ED frequent flyers in order to facilitate interventions targeted at reducing the number of frequent flyers and as a result, the amount of ED overcrowding. Identifying groups of ED frequent flyers and their specific needs will help reduce ongoing UCI ED costs and resources and allow ED doctors to better focus their care on patients in a life or death condition.
